In the real world, we have to repeat things that we do a lot.

For example, think about your day! You might wake up, get ready, go to work or school, come home, eat dinner, get ready for bed, and go to sleep. You repeat these things every day!

One of the really cool things that we can do with computers is to repeat tasks a lot of times.

For example, imagine that you want the computer to draw a square, but you want it to draw the square 100 times.

You could tell the computer to draw the square, and then tell it again, and then tell it again, up to 100 times - or you could use something called a loop.

A loop is something that you can use to tell the computer to repeat a task a lot of times.

So, instead of telling the computer to draw a square over and over, you could just say something like "draw a square 100 times" and the computer would draw the square 100 times!

Loops are good because we only have to give the computer instructions once. We don't have to type out "draw a square draw a square draw a square" 100 times. We just have to say "draw a square 100 times" and the computer knows what to do.

This means that, if there are mistakes in our instructions, we can find and fix them easily.
